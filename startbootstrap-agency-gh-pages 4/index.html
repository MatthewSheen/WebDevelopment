What to Be Aware Of...

Although beautiful, it can be dangerous.

Pickpocketing

This is common in tourist-heavy areas like the Eiffel Tower as well as crowded metro stations. Theives work in groups and can be very skilled. Always keep your belongings secure and be aware of your surroundings.

Scams

Various scams like to target tourists and they have many tricks. It's best to politely decline offers from strangers and avoid engaging with anyone who approaches you with these ploys.

Traffic and Road Safety

Traffic in Paris can be very chaotic and rules might be different from what you're used to. Be careful when crossing streets as vehicles might not stop. Remain alert when nagivating the streets.

History

Paris is home to a rich history

  • 3rd Century BC - 5th Century AD

    Antiquity

    Founding and Gallo-Roman Period. Paris was first settled by the Gallic tribe but was later conquered by the Romans in 52 BC. They built it into a propserous city with baths, theators, temples, and even an ampitheater.

  • 5th Century - 15th Century

    Middle Ages

    After the fall of the Roman Empire, Paris became the main scholarly and cultural center of Europe. The construction of the Notre-Dame and the University of Paris.

  • 16th Century - 18th Century

    Renaissance Period

    This was a period that saw Paris become a hub of commerce, finance, science, and the arts of Europe. The Louvre was a staple in this transition. The late 18th century marked the beginning of the French Revolution.

  • 19th Century - 20th Century

    Modern Era

    Paris underwent incredible transformations. With it came the introduction of the Eiffel Tower. the city continued to grow into a metropolis depsite going through multiple world wars.

Eiffel Tower

"La Dame de Fer"

The Eiffel Tower stands majestically over Paris with its incredible iron lattice design. It has become a symbol of France's creativity and ingenuity. Tourists are drawn to its breathtaking heights where they have the ability to view Paris's unparalleled views.

The Louvre

"Musée du Louvre"

The Louvre is not just a museum but an incredible palace of history that was once the residence of French kings. Visiotrs can marvel at world-famous masterpieces including the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo. The Louvre is a captivating journey through France's time and creativity.

Notre-Dame Cathedral

"Notre-Dame de Paris"

The Notre-Dame cathedral is a masterpiece of French architecture and a beacon of their culture that has stood for centuries. It's witnessed history, celebrated triumphs, and has been an inspriation of countless tales. Admire its stained glass windowns and incredibly scripted facade.

Sacré-Cœur Basilica

"Basilique du Sacré Cœur de Montmartre"

Standing on top of the Montmartre hill, the Basilica offers a breathtaking panoramic of the views of Paris. It's incredible white domes and mosaics embody the Roman-Byzatine architecture which entices its visitors. You can watch the sunset of Paris while admiring Basilica's beauty.

Arc du Triomphe

"Arc de Triomphe de l'Étoile"

The Arc du Triomphe is a monument at the heart of Paris. It honors those who fought and died in the Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars of France. It stands at the top of the Champs-Élysées and depicts victorious and patriotic battle scenes of France's history.

Musée d'Orsay

"d'Orsay"

The Musée d'Orsay is a stunning railway station on the banks of the Seine. It depicts the world's largest collection of impressionist and post-impressionist masterpeices which include those from Monet and Van Gogh. It offers an insight into the most influential art movements of the 19th and 20th centuries and a view of Paris from the upper floors.
